Approach

How Starts with Soap works.

1.         Starts with Soap is, in principle, a grant-giving organization.

2.         Annually, scholars will work within their communities to fundraise money that will go into a national fund.

3.         Those same scholars will preform need assessments within their communities: consulting with schools, community-based service, aid organizations etc to identity the specific needs of the education system in their area.

4.         Scholars will then create a proposal—write a grant—that outlines a plan of action to address the most pressing needs in their community based on their need assessments and the cost to address that need.

5.         The grant will be approved in collaboration with the scholar and the board of directors. Money will then be distributed based on the proposal the scholar submitted. This is flexible: the scholar may arrange for the money to be donated directly to a school, to a non profit that does service in their community, to an approved non profit or to the scholar themselves if they are planning to carry out a service project to address the need themselves.

Mission

To provide equal access to basic public health and sanitation needs in educational institutions.

  • Impetus

    Many schools that are on the lower end of the socioeconomic scale still suffer from the lack of basic public health resources such as soap and soap dispensers. This issue not only affects the quality of an educational institution but also the safety and healthiness of its members as they are unable to prevent sickness from sanitation related health hazards.

  • Purpose

    Our purpose is to eliminate threats to the healthiness of students due to underfunded facilities and lack of sanitation resources in educational institutions across the nation.

  • Goals

    Our goal is to supply local communities with the necessary resources and funds to ensure proper sanitation levels and promote healthiness in their members.

  • Execution

    Our funding comes from a combination of obtaining grants and fundraising privately. We plan to align with the United Way organization to create a network of supporters and ambassadors throughout local communities and then distribute the collected funds to respective communities on a need-based system. The majority of our advertising is done through word-of-mouth, such as setting up initiatives on college campuses, and over social media, where we currently maintain a Facebook, Instagram and Twitter page.
