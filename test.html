    Who We Are

    Starts With Soap is a 501(c)3 charitable organization that works to promote equitable education to our nation’s most underfunded schools by providing soap and other necessary materials that they are otherwise unable to afford.

    Our mission is to promote equitable education in our nation’s most underfunded schools providing basic necessities through innovative service projects.



    Approach

    Steps to Start with Soap.

    Grassroots Relationships

    Chapters partner with local underfunded schools to continuously provide the necessities they lack.

    Support Infrastructure

    A strong regional support network allows chapters to make the most of their resources and share ideas.

    National Initiatives

    National Initiatives allow SWS to effect broad and long-lasting change at a large scale.

            

            We recognize that change starts at the grassroots level, and we’ve organized our charity with this in mind.

            High schools around the nation have Starts With Soap chapters that partner with an underfunded school to act as a supplier for soap, as well as other unmet necessities.

            The activities of these high school and college chapters are coordinated and overseen by a team of regional executives and regional managers.

            The activities of regional executives and managers by our national leadership. Our national leadership team also manages fundraising, marketing, and outreach operations on a national level.

    Corridor of Shame

    Along the I-95 corridor in rural South Carolina, nearly 132,000 students attend schools that are underfunded to the point that their status and condition are illegal. This neglect has been contested and stalled in the South Carolina Supreme Court for nearly 20 years, and the delay in legal matters has been forcing an educational burden on the students who reside in the Corridor.

    Currently, students are suffering every day and unable to reap the benefits of a strong education. Starts with Soap is currently working with a South Carolina-based NGO, Student Space, to reduce the plight by providing vital supplies to schools within the Corridor of Shame. Our goal is to target multiple schools in the area and assess the true needs of the schools by communicating with teachers and staff close to the heart of the issue. The project’s efforts are presently being led by Merrit of Student Space and Shrey, SWS Regional Executive.

    For more information, email: sagarwal@startswithsoap.org

    Detroit

    SWS Regional Executive Rose is leading the institution of a new 90 minute reading program for Bennett Elementary School in Detroit, in order to provide students of lower income backgrounds a stronger background in reading.

    SWS, with the support of the Detroit Country Day Junior School Faculty and Betsy A. VanDeusen, Ph.D Department Chairperson, Teacher Education and Professional Development at Central Michigan University, develops a reading curriculum that targets 3rd graders of underprivileged schools. The purpose of the program is to help ensure that the 800 books that SWS will donate to Bennett are to be used in the most effective way possible. Key components of the program include vocabulary enrichment, passage comprehension, and development of independent reading skills. To be monitored by the University of Michigan SWS Chapter, the program is set to be piloted in the Spring and officially implemented in Fall 2017.

    For more information, email: rwong@startswithsoap.org

    Atlanta

    SWS is returning to the source of our inspiration, Parklane Elementary School, in Atlanta. All students attending the primary school participate in the Free and Reduced Lunch Program, indicating that each is of a low-income background and doesn’t often receive a number of opportunities to grow from. SWS Regional Executive Alex has been communicating with the school to help them install a STEM Education Learning Lab by Fall 2016. The station will be constructed using a Makey Makey compatible product, encouraging direct student engagement with technological and scientific concepts. We are currently seeking out a constant SWS Representative in the Atlanta area to monitor progress of the project.

    For more information, email: azhang@startswithsoap.org

    Indianapolis

    Living in the age of advancement, we’re all quite familiar with the integration that technology has in our daily lives, especially in learning environments. Starts with Soap is addressing this concern head on with Computer Literacy for Indy, under the direction of SWS Regional Executive Vikas. The program is aimed towards advancing equitable computer literacy education among students of the Indianapolis Public School Districts via funding for the Edna Martin Christian Center. The Center currently serves 300 unduplicated students from these districts by providing tutoring services, after-school care, and supplementary educational resources. Our goal is to provide a set of computers to the Center, equipped with learning software that will teach both staff and students the workings of the technology. We are working alongside a nationally-recognized software company to arrange for the implementation of Computer Literacy for Indy. Follow along as we make a difference in the lives of countless students!

    For more information, email: vmaturi@startswithsoap.org

    West Virginia

    SWS is planning to help the victims of the recent West Virginia Floods under the lead of SWS Regional Executive Caroline. Our mission is to help rebuild schools through the re-implementation of basic supplies once the media has left and the real clean up begins. Caroline has been in contact with a West Virginia school in need of restock prior to the start of the school year, and SWS will follow up with a project with the intention of focusing on the school’s most demonstrated need. Our efforts will culminate through a three-pronged approach targeting fundraising for supplies while engendering awareness in our communities, both state- and nationwide!

    Deland Florida

    In Spring of 2016, Volusia County implemented a new uniform policy for its schools, one of which was Starke Elementary in Deland, FL. A majority of students qualify for FRLP (99% of student body), and many could not support the new costs that a school uniform would bring in. After seeing a video released by Starke, SWS decided to lend a hand in an effort led by SWS Regional Exec Dalton. Dalton raised funds for a donation 143 uniforms and 631 school supplies for the students living in Deland, FL. Before school started in 2016, SWS delivered the donation to Starke Elementary administration; it was met with great excitement from the staff and students' families, who are now able to watch their children succeed in a new setting. Watch the journey and an interview with the staff: https://m.youtube.com/watch?v=t715FjEM98U
